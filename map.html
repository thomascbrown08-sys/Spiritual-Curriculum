Curriculum Map

Nine modules, one sequence, multiple entry points

The inside-out logic of this sequence: feel the need → encounter the story that meets it → examine the document that tells it → encounter the fact that anchors it → trust the honesty of that document → look at what suffering does to faith and what faith does to suffering → look for its fruit → step through the door → live it out.

Modules can be taken in sequence or selected individually. Each stands alone. Together they form a cumulative case that no single module carries by itself.

The Modules at a Glance

Module 1

The Honest Mirror

What we were made for, and what we have turned away from. The beauty of the Triune God; the tragedy of the turn; sin as power, guilt, and shame; the gospel as news rather than advice.
Module 2

The Compelling Story

Why the gospel has the shape of truth. The story no one would have invented. The Prodigal Son as a window into God's character. The Spirit working through narrative.
Module 3

The Examined Record

Prophecy, archaeology, and the historical reliability of Scripture. Isaiah 53. The Tel Dan Stele. Why this document deserves serious intellectual engagement.
Module 4 · New

The Resurrection

The most disruptive fact in history. The historical case, the existential stakes, the personal presence of the risen Christ. What it means to live as if this is true.
Module 5

The Unvarnished Record

Scripture's radical honesty about human and institutional failure. Psalm 88 — the psalm that ends in darkness. The prophets against power. Why this honesty is itself evidence.
Module 6 · New

The Problem of Suffering

The intellectual and personal problem of pain. The cross as God entering suffering rather than explaining it. Lament as a theological category. What we can and cannot promise.
Module 7

Signposts of Growth

Seeing God's faithfulness over time. Fruit as expression, not measure. Why inconsistency is a call to return to the vine, not a verdict on whether we were ever connected.
Module 8

Obedience as the Door

John 7:17 in practice. Obedience as natural expression and explicit command. Anchored obedience. Unanswered prayer and the Spirit's intercession. The living experiment.
Module 9

Living It Out

Faith that moves the hands and feet. The Sermon on the Mount. Homecoming and cost — both are true. The church as the visible shape of Trinitarian love in the world.

The Sequence Logic

Phase One: The Need and the Story (Modules 1–2)

The curriculum begins with the most personally immediate questions: does this document describe me accurately? And does the story it tells have the shape of something true? No prior commitment to Scripture's authority is required — only honesty about one's own experience, and openness to encountering a narrative. These two modules create the felt need and the initial draw that make the evidential modules personally motivated rather than academically remote.

Phase Two: The Evidence (Modules 3–5)

Having been drawn in by the story, the group's natural question becomes: can I trust the document that tells it? Modules 3 and 5 approach this from outside the text (prophecy, archaeology, historical reliability) and from inside it (Scripture's own unflinching honesty about failure). Module 4 addresses the central historical claim directly: the resurrection, which is the load-bearing fact of the entire structure. Together these three modules make a two-and-a-half session case for why this document deserves sustained, serious attention.

Phase Three: Suffering and Reality (Module 6)

Before moving to the applied material of the later modules, the curriculum pauses to address the most significant intellectual and personal barrier most Western post-Christian people carry: the problem of suffering and evil. This module is placed here deliberately — after the evidence has been examined and before the call to faith is issued, because it would be dishonest to issue that call without acknowledging what it costs and what it asks of people who carry real wounds. This module is the curriculum's most pastorally demanding and most important for people in genuine pain.

Phase Four: The Applied Life (Modules 7–9)

With the case established and the hard questions addressed, the final three modules turn to what following this actually produces in a life: observable growth over time (Module 7), the practice of obedience as John 7:17's living experiment (Module 8), and the full texture of a life shaped by the resurrection and lived in community (Module 9). These modules are the destination the earlier ones point toward — and Modules 8 and 9 in particular will continue to grow as the group brings its own experience to bear on them.

🧭 For Facilitators: Module Selection

If your group cannot commit to all nine sessions, here are suggested short sequences:

Four-session essentials: Modules 1, 2, 4, 9. This covers the diagnosis, the story, the resurrection, and the destination.

Apologetics focus: Modules 3, 4, 5, 6. Historical reliability, the resurrection, Scripture's honesty, and suffering — the four most common intellectual barriers.

Pastoral focus: Modules 1, 6, 7, 8. The honest diagnosis, the problem of suffering, the signposts of growth, and the practice of obedience. Best for groups that already have some faith background and are working through doubt or spiritual stagnation.

For the wounded: Modules 1, 5, 6, and the Spine. Begin with honest diagnosis, move through Scripture's own honesty about darkness, and spend significant time on suffering before anything else. The Spine's material on Christological assurance is essential for this group.
