A Curriculum for Examined Faith

Seeing and Believing

A small group curriculum for those beginning the movement toward faith, and those who have already begun and want to understand what they are moving toward. It draws on historical, psychological, literary, moral, and personal evidence — because the case for Christian faith is not thin enough to rest on just one.

"If anyone's will is to do God's will, he will know whether the teaching is from God."
— John 7:17

What This Curriculum Is

Most introductions to Christian faith start with an argument: weigh the reasons, evaluate the facts, and then decide. This curriculum does not avoid honest argument — but it rests on a deeper observation drawn from John 7:17: understanding follows the intent to obey. Sight follows the first step toward the light.

This is not anti-intellectual. It is a claim about how spiritual perception works — one that every serious Christian across the centuries has reported from experience: faith is not merely a conclusion you reach but a direction you begin to move in. A Belief with Intent, prompted and enabled by the Holy Spirit.

The organizing question of this curriculum is not merely Is Christianity true? but Is Christianity the kind of thing worth orienting your life around — and what happens when you begin to?

How to Navigate

This curriculum is structured as a set of linked documents. Each can be read independently, but they are designed to be traversed in sequence. Use the sidebar to move between sections at any time.

The Spine — The theological foundation: John 7:17, Belief with Intent, assurance, and the Holy Spirit's prior initiative. Begin here if you want to understand the architecture before the modules.

The Curriculum Map — An overview of all nine modules, their sequence logic, and how they connect. The best starting point for a facilitator planning a series.

The Modules — Nine sessions, each standing alone but designed to build on one another. Each module page is an outline and guide; full session documents are linked where available.

Glossary — Key terms used throughout the curriculum with precise definitions. Terms are cross-linked from module pages.

Resources — Consolidated deep dive suggestions for every module, organised by topic.

Using the View Controls

The panel in the upper right of every page lets you show or hide different kinds of content. Your preferences are saved across visits.

  • 🧭 Facilitator Notes — Guidance for those leading a group: what to watch for, what not to rush, pastoral cautions.
  • 📚 Deep Dives — Recommended reading and scholarly resources for those who want to go further.
  • 🌍 Cultural Context — Notes on how the material translates across different cultural backgrounds, including honor-shame frameworks.
  • ⚖️ Theological Nuance — Notes on points of genuine debate within Christian theology — where traditions differ and why.
  • 🕊️ Wounded Pilgrim — Material specifically addressed to those who have been hurt by the church, those who tried and found the fountain dry, or those wondering if the door is still open after a long absence.

A Note on Tone

This curriculum assumes good faith — that everyone engaging with it is genuinely seeking, whether from inside faith or outside it. Disagreement is welcome. Certainty is not required. The goal is honest engagement with the material, not performance of belief.

It is also written for real people in real conditions — including people who have been disappointed by the church, people in seasons of spiritual dryness, and people who have prayed many times and wonder whether it has counted. That material is not hidden. It is here, throughout, marked with the 🕊️ Wounded Pilgrim marker, because the curriculum that cannot speak to those people is not telling the whole truth.

Questions, comments, and corrections are genuinely welcome — this curriculum is a living document and honest engagement with it makes it better. Please write to ThomasCBrown08@gmail.com.
