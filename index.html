Thinking Carefully About Christian Faith

Seeing and Believing

A resource for those beginning the movement toward faith, those who have already begun and want to understand what they are moving toward, and those who want to think more carefully about questions the church has not always handled well. It draws on historical, psychological, literary, moral, and personal evidence — because the case for Christian faith is not thin enough to rest on just one.

"If anyone's will is to do God's will, he will know whether the teaching is from God."
— John 7:17

What This Site Is

Most introductions to Christian faith start with an argument: weigh the reasons, evaluate the facts, and then decide. This site does not avoid honest argument — but it rests on a deeper observation drawn from John 7:17: understanding follows the intent to obey. Sight follows the first step toward the light.

This is not anti-intellectual. It is a claim about how spiritual perception works — one that every serious Christian across the centuries has reported from experience: faith is not merely a conclusion you reach but a direction you begin to move in. A Belief with Intent, prompted and enabled by the Holy Spirit.

The organizing question is not merely Is Christianity true? but Is Christianity the kind of thing worth orienting your life around — and what happens when you begin to?

At its core is a nine-module curriculum designed to be read by individuals or used in small groups — a guided journey through the need, the evidence, the hard questions, and the lived practice of Christian faith. It is built especially for those navigating faith outside of a church community: people who are searching, returning, or rebuilding their relationship with God without a gathered context to do it in. It also works well in small group settings, and it hosts standalone resources that go deeper on topics that deserve more careful exploration than a single module can provide: the gospel itself, theological questions where thoughtful Christians disagree, and practical guidance for those beginning or returning to the life of faith.

How to Navigate

The site is structured as a set of linked documents. The curriculum modules can be read independently, but they are designed to be traversed in sequence. Use the sidebar to move between sections at any time.

The Spine — The theological foundation: John 7:17, Belief with Intent, assurance, and the Holy Spirit's prior initiative. Begin here if you want to understand the architecture before the modules.

The Curriculum Map — An overview of all ten modules, their sequence logic, and how they connect. The best starting point for a facilitator planning a series.

The Modules — Ten sessions, each standing alone but designed to build on one another. Modules 1–9 cover the case for Christian faith and the shape of a life that follows from it. Module 10 addresses church conflict: the Enemy's specific tactical patterns for fracturing Christian community, named and equipped for the peacemaker.

Responding to the Gospel — For those who want to understand what it means to respond to what they have encountered here. Not an assumption that every reader needs this — but a careful, honest resource for those who are ready, including those who have responded before and wonder whether it counted.

Glossary — Key terms used throughout the site with precise definitions. Terms are cross-linked from module pages.

Resources — Consolidated deep dive suggestions for every module, organised by topic.

🕊️ The Wounded Pilgrim — A dedicated space for those who have been hurt by the church, those in a long season of spiritual dryness, and those who wonder if the door is still open. Not a module — a place set apart, addressed directly to the person who needs it most.

About — What this curriculum is, how it was made, and why. Includes a note from the author for those who want to understand the personal circumstances that shaped it.

A Note on Tone

This curriculum assumes good faith — that everyone engaging with it is genuinely seeking, whether from inside faith or outside it. Disagreement is welcome. Certainty is not required. The goal is honest engagement with the material, not performance of belief.

It is written for real people in real conditions — including people who have been disappointed by the church, people in seasons of spiritual dryness, and people who have prayed many times and wonder whether it has counted. That material is not hidden. It is present throughout every module, marked with the 🕊️ Wounded Pilgrim marker. There is also a dedicated page for those who need it most — not a module, but a place set apart.

For more about this curriculum's origins, methods, and the personal circumstances behind it, see the About page. Questions and corrections are always welcome there.
