Thinking Carefully About Christian Faith

Seeing and Believing

A resource for those beginning the movement toward faith, those who have already begun and want to understand what they are moving toward, and those who want to think more carefully about questions the church has not always handled well. It draws on historical, psychological, literary, moral, and personal evidence — because the case for Christian faith is not thin enough to rest on just one.

"If anyone's will is to do God's will, he will know whether the teaching is from God."
— John 7:17

What This Site Is

Most introductions to Christian faith start with an argument: weigh the reasons, evaluate the facts, and then decide. This site does not avoid honest argument — but it rests on a deeper observation drawn from John 7:17: understanding follows the intent to obey. Sight follows the first step toward the light.

This is not anti-intellectual. It is a claim about how spiritual perception works — one that every serious Christian across the centuries has reported from experience: faith is not merely a conclusion you reach but a direction you begin to move in. A Belief with Intent, prompted and enabled by the Holy Spirit.

The organizing question is not merely Is Christianity true? but Is Christianity the kind of thing worth orienting your life around — and what happens when you begin to?

At its core is a nine-module curriculum designed for small groups — a guided journey through the need, the evidence, the hard questions, and the lived practice of Christian faith. But the site also hosts standalone resources that go deeper on topics that deserve more careful exploration than a single module can provide: the gospel itself, theological questions where thoughtful Christians disagree, and practical guidance for those beginning or returning to the life of faith.

How to Navigate

The site is structured as a set of linked documents. The curriculum modules can be read independently, but they are designed to be traversed in sequence. Use the sidebar to move between sections at any time.

The Spine — The theological foundation: John 7:17, Belief with Intent, assurance, and the Holy Spirit's prior initiative. Begin here if you want to understand the architecture before the modules.

The Curriculum Map — An overview of all nine modules, their sequence logic, and how they connect. The best starting point for a facilitator planning a series.

The Modules — Nine sessions, each standing alone but designed to build on one another. Each module page is an outline and guide; full session documents are linked where available.

Responding to the Gospel — For those who want to understand what it means to respond to what they have encountered here. Not an assumption that every reader needs this — but a careful, honest resource for those who are ready, including those who have responded before and wonder whether it counted.

Glossary — Key terms used throughout the site with precise definitions. Terms are cross-linked from module pages.

Resources — Consolidated deep dive suggestions for every module, organised by topic.

Using the View Controls

  • 🧭 Facilitator Notes — Guidance for those leading a group: what to watch for, what not to rush, pastoral cautions.
  • 📚 Deep Dives — Recommended reading and scholarly resources for those who want to go further.
  • 🌍 Cultural Context — Notes on how the material translates across different cultural backgrounds, including honor-shame frameworks.
  • ⚖️ Theological Nuance — Notes on points of genuine debate within Christian theology — where traditions differ and why.
  • 🕊️ Wounded Pilgrim — Material specifically addressed to those who have been hurt by the church, those who tried and found the fountain dry, or those wondering if the door is still open after a long absence.

A Note on Tone

This curriculum assumes good faith — that everyone engaging with it is genuinely seeking, whether from inside faith or outside it. Disagreement is welcome. Certainty is not required. The goal is honest engagement with the material, not performance of belief.

It is also written for real people in real conditions — including people who have been disappointed by the church, people in seasons of spiritual dryness, and people who have prayed many times and wonder whether it has counted. That material is not hidden. It is here, throughout, marked with the 🕊️ Wounded Pilgrim marker, because the curriculum that cannot speak to those people is not telling the whole truth.

This curriculum began as one person's attempt to think carefully about faith, doubt, and the evidence for Christian belief. The tools used in its preparation include standard theological reference works, academic scholarship, and AI assistance (Claude, developed by Anthropic) for research and drafting. Every section has been reviewed by the author and is intended to be reviewed by theologically mature readers before broader use. AI assistance introduces the possibility of errors — in theological nuance, source attribution, or pastoral judgment — that careful human review is designed to catch.

Questions, comments, and corrections are genuinely welcome — this curriculum is a living document and honest engagement with it makes it better. If you find something that needs fixing, your response is not just welcome but needed. Please write to ThomasCBrown08@gmail.com.
